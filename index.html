About

I’m a PhD candidate working at the intersection of numerical reservoir simulation, inverse problems, and machine learning. My current focus is the discrete adjoint method for two-phase flow and how it can be paired with modern parameterizations — PCA, VAE, and direct grid-cell representations — to history-match channelized reservoirs.

Before my PhD, I worked on field-scale modelling and reservoir characterization. I write most of my research code from scratch in Python so I understand every line of the gradient that ends up in the optimizer.

Research

Discrete Adjoint for Two-Phase Flow

A from-scratch Python simulator with a hand-derived discrete adjoint, used to compute exact gradients of mismatch functionals with respect to millions of grid-cell parameters in seconds.

Deep Generative Priors for Channelized Reservoirs

Comparing direct, PCA, and VAE-based parameterizations on the same gradient-based history-matching loop — and showing when the geological prior actually pays off.

Field-Scale Inversion

Scaling adjoint-driven HM workflows from synthetic 1D and 3D cases to full field models with wells, faults, and realistic production constraints.
